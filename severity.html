Humanitarian corridors under siege: a daily severity model of civilian evacuation from Mariupol

The severity axis of a three-axis evacuation decision matrix (Severity × Feasibility × Destination viability), computed for each day of the siege window, 5 March – 20 May 2022, from ACLED event data, ERA5-referenced temperature, the documented corridor record, and WorldPop demographics. Select a date in §2 to generate the situation assessment in force that day.

RQ · How can and should AI tools be used to enhance the protection and evacuation of civilians during armed conflict, and how do they support or challenge obligations under IHL?

Fig. 2 — Evacuation corridors from Mariupol, March–May 2022. Hatched area: territory under Russian control (approximate, mid-March); dashed line: approximate front line. The corridor corresponding to the regime selected in §1 is emphasised, and the inset reports the composite severity for the selected date with its component contributors; the encirclement ring around Mariupol is coloured and scaled by severity. Westbound movement to Zaporizhzhia crossed Russian-held territory and checkpoints between Manhush and Vasylivka; the eastbound route into Russian-controlled territory via Bezimenne filtration operated in parallel throughout all phases, and during the UN/ICRC operation some evacuees proceeded to Russian-controlled areas rather than Zaporizhzhia.
